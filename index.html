Methods

Wave height (Hs) is the model significant wave height; only the weekly aggregation is mine. Orbital velocity on the strandings chart is the near-bed max orbital velocity u = (πH/T)/sinh(kd) in cm/s, drawn as a band spanning the 16–27 m habitat depths (top edge = 16 m, faster; lower edge = 27 m). The peak band uses storm-peak-hour swell; the median band uses the week's median swell.

Wave energy flux: P ≈ 0.49·Hs²·T kW/m (Te≈T proxy, slight over-estimate). Fetch (JONSWAP, from wind-sea): F = (U²/g)[g·Hm0/(0.0016·U²)]².

Orbital motion (linear theory, bed at z=−d):

A=(H/2)cosh[k(z+d)]/sinh(kd) u=(πH/T)cosh[k(z+d)]/sinh(kd) u_s=(H/2)²ωk cosh[2k(z+d)]/(2 sinh² kd) (Stokes drift, net)

Closed orbits give zero net transport; net along-swell push is the Stokes drift. Habitat is 16–27 m; the depth slider also reaches 2 m to model animals carried into the shallows, where orbital velocities rise sharply (linear theory becomes an upper bound there as waves shoal/break).

Sources. Strandings & regions digitised from Booth, Trevor-Jones, Klanten & Beretta (2025), J. Fish Biol. doi:10.1111/jfb.70019. Wave: Open-Meteo Marine; wind: Open-Meteo ERA5; ~33.8°S, 151.4°E, modelled. In-situ alternative: MHL Sydney Directional Waverider (WAVESYD), AODN THREDDS.
